Biography

She transferred school from University of Seoul in Korea to Stony Brook University in NY in 2015 and changed her major from sculpture to studio art. It was a big turning point in her life and her artwork. She is now working to fuse many areas of arts. The questions she investigates her work come are fundamental. She questions the world, and her answer is melted into her artwork. Personal stories always inspire her and breathe in her work. Most of her artworks are about ‘double-sidedness’. It means having two aspects or characters. Sometime, it consists of opposite aspects or it is combined with dissimilar things. Her artworks are about that ‘double-sidedness.’ It can be many things existed in the world. She mentions about jellyfish to explain this concept. This creature has so beautiful appearance but it is dangerous creature. Like its beautiful appearance, it has incredible movement. Movement of jellyfish seems like it is in a weightless state. It brought her another dissimilar concept. That is space that has zero gravity and aurora that has similar movement of jellyfish. She combines these mismatched things called ‘double-sidedness’ and puts these together in her works. When someone sees her work, they can't easily imagine what it is, and what she want to show. But, people can imagine and feel it through her work.
